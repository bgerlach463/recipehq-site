Our Story

Built out of frustration with wasted groceries.

RecipeHQ started with a simple problem: we'd plan a week of dinners, buy all the ingredients, and then life would happen. A spontaneous dinner out, a busy Wednesday, a late meeting — and suddenly that bunch of cilantro, the fresh salmon, the spinach we had big plans for were quietly going bad in the fridge.

The apps we tried were built around collecting recipes, not cooking them. They had beautiful interfaces and zero help with the actual problem: planning meals that made sense together, tracking what we had on hand, and not throwing $50 of groceries away every week.

So we built RecipeHQ — a tool focused squarely on home cooks who buy fresh ingredients and care about not wasting them. No social features, no algorithmic recipe feeds, no ads. Just a genuinely useful kitchen companion.

It's built in Pittsburgh, PA, and used daily in our own kitchen. We hope it earns a place in yours.

What we believe

Less waste, more flavor

Planning around ingredient overlap doesn't mean boring meals. It means smarter shopping and more creative cooking.

Tools should stay out of your way

RecipeHQ takes 5 minutes to set up and then quietly does its job. No daily habit streaks, no social pressure.

Your data is yours

We don't sell data, serve ads, or train AI models on your recipes. We make money from subscriptions — that's it.

Built by cooks, for cooks

Every feature in this app exists because we needed it ourselves. If it doesn't help in a real kitchen, it doesn't ship.
