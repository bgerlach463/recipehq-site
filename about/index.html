About MyRecipeHQ

Built out of frustration with wasted groceries.

MyRecipeHQ started with a simple problem: I'd plan a week of dinners, buy all the ingredients, and then life would happen. A spontaneous dinner out, a busy Wednesday, a late meeting — and suddenly that bunch of cilantro, the fresh salmon, the spinach I had big plans for were quietly going bad in the fridge.

The apps I tried were built around collecting recipes, not cooking them. They had beautiful interfaces and zero help with the actual problem: planning meals that made sense together, tracking what I had on hand, and not throwing $50 of groceries away every week.

So I built MyRecipeHQ — a tool focused squarely on home cooks who buy fresh ingredients and care about not wasting them. No social features, no algorithmic recipe feeds. Just a genuinely useful kitchen companion.

It's used daily in my own kitchen. I hope it earns a place in yours.

What I believe

Less waste, more flavor

Planning around ingredient overlap doesn't mean boring meals. It means smarter shopping and more creative cooking.

Tools should stay out of your way

MyRecipeHQ takes minutes to get started and then quietly does its job. No daily habit streaks, no social pressure.

Your data is yours

MyRecipeHQ doesn't sell data or train AI models on your recipes. Your data stays yours.

Built by a home cook, for home cooks

Every feature in this app exists because I needed it myself. If it doesn't help in a real kitchen, it doesn't ship.

Brad Gerlach
Founder
